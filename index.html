Key Insight — 2025 NYTS FDA CTP PUF Weighted Analysis: The e-cigarette youth market has undergone 4 regime changes in 6 years: JUUL (2019-2020) → Puff Bar (2021-2022) → Elf Bar (2023-2024) → Geek Bar (2025, 64.1% contact rate). Geek Bar was not even in the 2024 top 5, yet captured 64.1% of youth users in 2025 — the fastest brand takeover in NYTS history. Elf Bar continues declining (56.7% → 36.1% → 21.5%). New entrants Lost Mary (19.8%) and Raz (19.4%) also surged. Only 2 of the 2025 top 10 (Vuse #5, JUUL #9) hold FDA PMTA authorization. Disposables now account for 66.3% of e-cig device types (↑10.7pp from 2024). Daily use: 27.5%; frequent use (20-30 days): 41.2%.

📐 Methodology — How is this calculated?
Framework: Simplified PAF × DALY population health impact model — the standard framework used in FDA PMTA Section 5 (Population Health Impact Modeling).

Formula:
1. Smokers = Adult Population × Smoking Rate (WHO GHO data)
2. Switchers = Smokers × Switch Rate (%) (user input)
3. Harm Reduction % = (1 − Relative Harm %) × 100
4. Avoided DALYs = Switchers × 0.12 × avgPAF × (1 − RelHarm/100) × Years
where 0.12 ≈ baseline annual DALYs per smoker (GBD), and avgPAF ≈ 0.50 averaged across 6 tobacco-related diseases.

PAF values used (GBD/WHO): Lung Cancer 0.85 · COPD 0.75 · Esophageal Cancer 0.52 · Bladder Cancer 0.45 · Ischemic Heart Disease 0.25 · Stroke 0.18.

⚠️ Model limitations (must be disclosed for any PMTA submission):
• Baseline DALY/smoker is a constant — not stratified by age, sex, or smoking duration.
• PAFs are static — does not model age-dependent risk or post-cessation recovery curves.
• Relative harm is a single scalar — no toxicology-weighted decomposition (NNK, TSNAs, aldehydes, etc.).
• Does not include initiation — FDA's net population benefit also requires subtracting harm from non-users newly attracted to the product.
• Linear model — real disease latency and dose-response are non-linear.

Use case: Directional estimate for early commercial assessment and pre-PMTA planning — not a regulatory-grade submission. For PMTA filings, extend to a full dynamic population model (e.g., SimSmoke framework, Levy et al. 2017) with age-cohort tracking and initiation/cessation flows.
