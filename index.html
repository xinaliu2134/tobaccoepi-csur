📐 Methodology — How is this calculated?
Framework: Simplified PAF × DALY population health impact model — the standard framework used in FDA PMTA Section 5 (Population Health Impact Modeling).

Formula:
1. Smokers = Adult Population × Smoking Rate (WHO GHO data)
2. Switchers = Smokers × Switch Rate (%) (user input)
3. Harm Reduction % = (1 − Relative Harm %) × 100
4. Avoided DALYs = Switchers × 0.12 × avgPAF × (1 − RelHarm/100) × Years
where 0.12 ≈ baseline annual DALYs per smoker (GBD), and avgPAF ≈ 0.50 averaged across 6 tobacco-related diseases.

PAF values used (GBD/WHO): Lung Cancer 0.85 · COPD 0.75 · Esophageal Cancer 0.52 · Bladder Cancer 0.45 · Ischemic Heart Disease 0.25 · Stroke 0.18.

⚠️ Model limitations (must be disclosed for any PMTA submission):
• Baseline DALY/smoker is a constant — not stratified by age, sex, or smoking duration.
• PAFs are static — does not model age-dependent risk or post-cessation recovery curves.
• Relative harm is a single scalar — no toxicology-weighted decomposition (NNK, TSNAs, aldehydes, etc.).
• Does not include initiation — FDA's net population benefit also requires subtracting harm from non-users newly attracted to the product.
• Linear model — real disease latency and dose-response are non-linear.

Use case: Directional estimate for early commercial assessment and pre-PMTA planning — not a regulatory-grade submission. For PMTA filings, extend to a full dynamic population model (e.g., SimSmoke framework, Levy et al. 2017) with age-cohort tracking and initiation/cessation flows.

PMTA Roadmap

FDA Premarket Tobacco Application — 6 Phases from Pre-Submission to Post-Market · 21 U.S.C. § 387j · 21 CFR Part 1114
APPH Standard: All PMTAs must demonstrate the product is "Appropriate for the Protection of Public Health" — net population benefit (adult user benefit from reduced harm/switching) outweighs population risk (youth initiation). Requires weighing totality of evidence across clinical, toxicological, behavioral, and epidemiological domains.
① Pre-Submission
⏱ 6–18 months
  • FDA Type B Pre-Sub meeting (strongly recommended)
  • Protocol alignment with CTP
  • Study design finalization
  • Manufacturing readiness
  • Lab selection (ISO 17025 required)
② Application Prep
⏱ 12–36 months
  • Clinical studies: AUS, BOE, switching
  • Toxicology & HPHC characterization
  • Consumer perception (TPPI)
  • Label comprehension / human factors
  • Manufacturing validation
  • All 11 sections + labeling package
③ Submission & Filing
⏱ 1–3 months
  • Submit via FDA Form 4057/4057a
  • Acceptance (RTA) review — format/completeness
  • Filing review — substantive threshold
  • 30-day public comment period opens
  • RTF = refuse to file; RTA = refuse to accept
④ Scientific Review
⏱ 12–48 months*
  • 12 parallel discipline reviews
  • Chemistry · Toxicology · Clinical pharmacology
  • Epidemiology · Behavioral pharmacology · Stats
  • Consumer perception · Engineering · Oncology
  • Amendments may restart 180-day clock
  • TPSAC referral (optional, advisory only)
⑤ Decision
⏱ Stat. 180 days*
  • ✅ MGO — product may be marketed under stated conditions
  • ❌ MDO — with specific denial grounds; challengeable in federal court
  • MDO applicants may resubmit with additional data
  • *Stat. clock routinely paused; real time = 13–58 months
⑥ Post-Market
⏱ Ongoing
  • Semi-annual/annual periodic reports (60-day deadline)
  • Serious AE: report within 15 calendar days
  • 30-day pre-notification for new advertising (first 6 mo)
  • Post-market studies (per MGO conditions)
  • MGO revocable if APPH standard no longer met

⚠️ Common MDO Reasons — Why Applications Fail:
  • Flavored products — insufficient comparative switching evidence: FDA's "fatal flaw" standard: flavored ENDS must show added adult benefit outweighing youth initiation risk. ~55,000 MDOs for fruit/candy/sweet products (Aug 2021); ~6,500 additional flavored MDOs subsequently. New flavor framework (Mar 2026): sliding-scale burden — fruit/candy highest, menthol/mint moderate.
  • Toxicological data gaps (Section 7): JUUL's 2022 MDO cited "insufficient and conflicting genotoxicity data and potentially harmful chemicals" — ISO 17025 accreditation and complete HPHC package are non-negotiable; conflicting data between studies is a fatal flaw.
  • Insufficient youth cross-appeal analysis: Consumer perception (TPPI) studies must cover youth populations; missing or inadequate youth appeal quantification for non-tobacco flavors.
  • Missing or inadequate Actual Use Study: No real-world switching data = no adult benefit evidence = MDO; AUS must reflect intended population at intended conditions of use.
  • Manufacturing deficiencies: Battery safety data gaps; inadequate design specifications; stability testing deficiencies; product description inconsistencies.
